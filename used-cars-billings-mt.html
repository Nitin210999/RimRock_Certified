Sales Tax

Most states impose a sales tax on used car purchases. The rate varies by state. For instance, Belgrade car dealerships in Montana might not charge sales tax on the car itself but you'd likely pay it when you register the car in your state.

Can You Return a Problematic Used Car?

When it comes to purchasing used cars in Billings, MT, it's natural to have concerns about the possibility of encountering problems with your vehicle. However, if you buy from a reputable dealer like Rimrock Certified Select, you can have peace of mind knowing that you have options if you do encounter issues with your purchase.

While many used cars are sold "as-is", there are protections in place for consumers, including the Montana used car lemon law. This law provides recourse for buyers who unknowingly purchase a problematic vehicle that fails to meet certain standards of quality or safety.

In Montana, the lemon law applies to used cars that are sold with an express warranty. If you purchase a used car from Rimrock Certified Select or another reputable dealer that comes with a warranty, and the vehicle develops significant defects covered by the warranty within a certain period after purchase, you may have the right to seek recourse under the lemon law.

However, it's important to note that the lemon law has specific criteria and procedures that must be followed. For example, the defects must substantially impair the use, value, or safety of the vehicle, and the dealer must be given a reasonable opportunity to repair the defects. If the seller is unable to resolve the issues after a reasonable number of attempts, you may be entitled to a refund or replacement vehicle under the lemon law.

While returning problematic used cars in Billings, MT might require several steps, especially if it falls under the lemon law, working with a reputable dealer like Rimrock Certified Select can make the experience smoother and more straightforward.
